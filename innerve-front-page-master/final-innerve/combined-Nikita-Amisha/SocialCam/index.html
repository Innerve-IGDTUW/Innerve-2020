Cyber SeekCure

Spreading awareness about Cyber Crimes

Innerve’20 launched its social campaign, Cyber SeekCure, on 25th September 2020. The campaign’s aim was to spread awareness about cyber crimes and the techniques with which netizens could secure their cyberspace. The Cyber Blog India was announced as the knowledge partner of our social campaign, CyberSeekCure. Innerve’20 expressed its heartfelt thanks to The Cyber Blog India for its support to this initiative to spread awareness about cyber security.

Innerve’20 as part of its social campaign reached out to people on the 28th of September on various social media platforms, inviting them to share their experience with cyber crime. These experiences were then shared with cyber security expert Raj Pagaria from Cyber Blog India, who discussed preventive measures against such crimes. The people who shared their story were given the option to stay anonymous and also to share these stories on our Instagram live session.

As we launched our social campaign, CyberSeekCure, we also released a video which presented our ideologies with regards to cybercrime in the guise of a satirical new channel report. The video contained some of the pressing problems arising due to cybercrime which we often tend to ignore.

Online Safet Eventy - Responsible Online Behaviour

Event conducted in association with Cyber Peace Foundation

The online world has both keyboard warriors and cyber trolls but it lacks responsible netizens. What we post online speaks volumes about who we really are. Innerve'20 in association with Cyber Peace Foundation conducted a webinar on Online Safety – Responsible Online Behaviour. The distinguished speaker was Janice Verghese, Cyber Lawyer, Cyber Security Specialist, Manager Communications and Training at Cyber Peace Foundation. The webinar was conducted on 1st October 2020 at 11:00 am. All attendees were given e-certificates.

Cyber Security Awareness Program

Event Conducted for School students

Under our social campaign, Cyber SeekCure, Innerve'20 organised a session on cyber security in various schools. The objective was to make students more aware about cyber crimes. Our speaker Janice Verghese, Cyber Lawyer, Cyber Security Specialist, Manager Communications and Training at Cyber Peace Foundation, sensitised the students about the threats online, talking about how they can safeguard themselves and their families online. We concluded on the note that cybersecurity is essential for one and all. The webinar was conducted on 8th October 2020 at 11:00 am. All attendees were given e-certificates.
